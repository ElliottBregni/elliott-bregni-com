This is the first post on a blog I've been meaning to start for years.

I spend most of my time on systems work — agent runtimes, real-time supply-chain
visibility, developer tooling. The kind of stuff where the interesting parts
rarely make it past internal Slack. This blog is where the parts I _can_ share
are going to live: things I learned in the open, mistakes that turned into
patterns, and short notes on tools I'd want to read about myself.

Topics I expect to write about:

- AI agent runtimes — capability gating, audit, why "secure by default" is hard
- MCP, RAG, and the long tail of LLM tool integrations
- Real-time data pipelines — what changes when seconds matter
- Postmortem-flavored notes from production incidents (sanitized, of course)
- Occasional rants about developer experience

Posts will be short by default. If something needs more room, it'll get more
room — but I'm trying to optimize for "publish, then expand" instead of
"draft for three weeks, never ship."

Subscribe via the RSS feed once it's wired up, or just check back. More soon.
